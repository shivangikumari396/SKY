Pneumonia

Overview

Pneumonia is a medical condition characterized by the inflammation of the air sacs (alveoli) in one or both lungs. This inflammation may lead to the accumulation of fluid or purulent material within the air sacs, resulting in symptoms such as a productive cough (phlegm or pus), fever, chills, and respiratory distress. The condition can be caused by various pathogens, including bacteria, viruses, and fungi. The severity of pneumonia ranges from mild cases to life-threatening situations. It poses a higher risk to specific populations, including infants, young children, older adults (above 65 years), and individuals with pre-existing health conditions or compromised immune systems. Proper medical evaluation and timely treatment are essential to manage and mitigate the impact of this potentially serious condition.

Symptoms

If you or someone you're with may be having a Pneumonia, pay particular attention to the time the symptoms began. Some treatment options are most effective when given soon after a Pneumonia begins. Signs and symptoms of Pneumonia include:

  • Chest pain: Discomfort or pain when breathing or coughing.
  • Shortness of breath: Difficulty in breathing or feeling breathless.
  • Cognitive changes: Confusion or alterations in mental awareness, particularly in older adults (65 years and above).
  • Fever, sweating, and chills: Elevated body temperature accompanied by sweating and shaking chills.
  • Lower body temperature: Often observed in older adults or those with weakened immune systems.
