Liver Disease

Overview

The liver, approximately the size of a football, is located just beneath the rib cage on the right side of the abdomen. It plays a crucial role in vital bodily functions, including the digestion of food and the elimination of toxic substances from the body. Liver diseases can be inherited through genetic predisposition. However, liver damage may also occur due to various external factors, such as viral infections, excessive alcohol consumption, and obesity. Prolonged exposure to such damaging conditions can result in scarring of the liver tissue, known as cirrhosis. This condition can progress to liver failure, which is potentially life-threatening. Early detection and treatment of liver disorders are essential to facilitate healing and prevent further complications. Effective management can often improve outcomes and preserve liver function.

Symptoms

If you or someone you're with may be having a stroke, pay particular attention to the time the symptoms began. Some treatment options are most effective when given soon after a stroke begins. Signs and symptoms of stroke include:

  • Jaundice: Yellowish discoloration of the skin and eyes.
  • Abdominal discomfort: Pain and swelling in the abdomen.
  • Edema: Swelling in the legs and ankles
  • Urine and stool changes: Dark-colored urine and pale stool.
  • Gastrointestinal symptoms: Nausea or vomiting.
